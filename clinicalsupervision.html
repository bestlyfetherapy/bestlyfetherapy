Clinical Supervision

Clinical Supervision is the professional relationship between a supervisor and supervisee. It is the process in which a Licensed Clinical Social Worker monitors and evaluates the supervisee’s clinical practice in order to increase their skills, gain confidence and competence as a social worker, and support them in providing quality and ethical social work services to their clients.

Our time together in supervision can be used to review your clinical cases, discuss clinical concerns or questions you are experiencing, and learn important clinical skills to prepare you to become a LCSW. We will have the opportunity to review the NASW Code of Ethics, prepare you for the ASWB exam, and explore a number of other important topics that are vital as social workers.

To be eligible for LCSW in the state of Virginia, one must receive a total of 100 hours of clinical supervision from a board approved clinical supervisor.

Please note that this process will done virtually.
